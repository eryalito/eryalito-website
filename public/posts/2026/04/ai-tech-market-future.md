# AI is Changing Tech Dev Markets - What Comes Next?

I was not expecting to start my blog journey with a post about AI. But here we are, as it was going to be inevitable, better to get it out of the way early.

## The Reality

As almost everyone in tech, I've been following the AI evolution relatively closely. The progress has been undoubtedly noticeable, especially in the last year. This is probably not related only to the models being "smarter", but the ecosystem around them growing: the RAGs, the MCPs, the fine-tuning tools, etc. It's not just the models, it's the whole infrastructure around them.

Maybe following the hype wave, or after a careful tradeoff analysis, companies are shifting their hiring policies in the Tech Spectrum. We're still in the moment in which companies are looking for people that can leverage AI tools, while others are full-on replacing some of the roles with AI Agents. It's clear today, just by reading job offers, what companies are leaning towards and which types of people are dictating the requirements.

## The Concerns

The first and most obvious concern to me is the impact this is having in the market. While companies and most enthusiasts are celebrating and claiming the end of the coding community, I think it's too bald to say that. This reminds me of the early days of the Internet, when the hype was that it would replace all traditional media, and instead we faced the dot-com bubble and a decade of slow growth; or the rise of the streaming platforms, when cinemas were supposed to die; or when wordpress made its debut, and everyone was saying that it would replace developers. I could keep going all day, but you got the point. The hype and the reality are often very different things, it's obviously hard to predict the future, and we're all probably wrong about what will actually happen anyways.

The second concern is about the long term, what the impact of this will be in the tech ecosystem. We're already seeing how "tech enthusiasts" and Cs without tech knowledge are taking over the market, and how the "tech people" are being pushed out. While seniors are shifting their focus, this is a big problem for new people joining the industry right now. The opportunities for juniors were shrinking since the pandemic explosion, and now with AI taking over, it's even worse. This is pushing people interested in tech to look for other industries, leaving behind a generational gap, and a lack of new and fresh ideas in the industry. This could be a serious problem in the long term, as the lack of new talent and ideas could lead to a stagnation of the industry, and a lack of innovation.

## The Future

Here it comes my hot take of the day. While AI will continue to grow for some months and probably a year or two, I think we're living in a hype train and it will derail. If there's not a MASSIVE breakthrough in the AI technology, I don't see a world in which the current tendency is sustainable. The costs of just running the models are already very high with current technology that needs constant supervision by humans, it's only reasonable to think that the costs will still be high in the future, especially with bigger and bigger models. 

We're already seeing the AI shift from general public to tech companies, as nobody is willing to pay a subscription that actually covers the costs of the models, let's not talk about generating revenue (let's be honest, most people wouldn't pay for a subscription just to avoid googling things). The ones that are willing to pay (and already paying) are the ones that plan to make money with it, which are tech companies/people. And we're seeing how almost all providers are increasing their prices, reducing the tokens, and adding more and more limitations to the usage. This is a clear sign that the current model is not sustainable, and it's only going to get worse.

Once the bubble bursts, I only see two possible scenarios: companies too deep in the AI scenario will have to throw insane amounts of money to keep it alive (do you remember when people said cloud services were cheaper??); or companies that are not that into it, that will shift again to the old paradigm and hire a lot of people back, which will be scarce and expensive. You see the trend, right? Either way, companies will have to pay more, and the ones that are not prepared for it will suffer the consequences.

At the end, looking at it with some perspective, I think AI will be a thing, but as it happened with any other thing in the past, it will be a tool that will be used by people, and not the other way around. The community will adapt, the technology will evolve, and there will be a new balance. The only question is how long it will take.
